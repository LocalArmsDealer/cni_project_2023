Should nuclear fission replace fossil fuels in the United States?

Written by Alina A. and Alexis C.


Introduction

Fossil fuels power most of the US. According to The Department of Energy, around 65% of energy production in 2021 was from fossil fuels, with the majority being from natural gas (graph below ↓↓↓). Of the remaining 35%, around 10% is nuclear power. Should that number be higher, is it high enough, or should it be lower? I don't have an answer, but I can provide information and a lot of sources.

Viewpoint 1: More Nuclear

Nuclear power produced 772 billion kWh (kilowatt hours) of power in 2022 according to the Energy Information Administration, which released a total of 0 p/kWh (pounds per kilowatt hour) of carbon. If that power were produced via natural gas, that would release 0.97 p/kWh (Source) for a total of approximately 374.42 million tons of carbon, all of which would be released into the atmosphere.
